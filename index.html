Is Privacy Shield dead yet?

(And by Privacy Shield I mean the transatlantic "agreement" supposedly regulating transatlantic data flows)

Not yet!

but it is really just a question of time

Current situation

The EU Commission issued a press release informing of the drafts of the legal texts that will put in place the EU-U.S. Privacy Shield adequacy decision on the level of data protection in the United States.

The U.S. Department of Commerce issued their own version of the texts, which differ substantially.

The EU MEP Jan Albrecht has deemed this new agreement mere "cosmetic changes", and the EU MEP Sophie 't Veld has highlighted several problems with it.

Privacy scholar Max Schrems has issued his own press release, saying that "they tried to put ten layers of lipstick on a pig" and backing those claims.

This adequacy decision should still be reviewed by the Article 29 Working Party, which will hopefully tear it to shreds.

Issues

bulk surveillance: the new agreement merely acknowledges mass surveillance, and carves many exceptions allowing it to take place, while the EUCJ sees bulk surveillance as damaging to the essence of privacy;

agreement: one side thinks this is an agreement, the other side views it as unilateral;

ombudsman: the ombudsman is really placed within the State Department, and not truly independent

redress mechanism: explicit limitations are placed on the acceptable global costs of the redress mechanisms, thereby limiting their functioning;

written assurance: this does not have force of law;

FAQ #

Why care about Privacy Shield? #

A few reasons:

  • The agreement weakens the protection of fundamental rights of EU citizens
  • It is impossible for an individual to enforce the agreement on their own
  • It favors US companies as they do not need in practice to respect the same level of privacy protection
  • Because these fundamental rights will become ever more important as we transition to a knowledge economy

How many Privacy Shield requests have been filed by EU citizens? #

Zero so far, but we can't wait!
