Is Safe Harbor dead yet?

(And by Safe Harbor I mean the International Safe Harbor Privacy Principles regulating transatlantic data flows)

YES!

but not yet in Switzerland

Current situation

The EU Court of Justice has decided on the case and invalidated Safe Harbor. The decision reads:

For all those reasons, the Court declares the Safe Harbour Decision invalid. This judgment has the consequence that the Irish supervisory authority is required to examine Mr Schrems’ complaint with all due diligence and, at the conclusion of its investigation, is to decide whether, pursuant to the directive, transfer of the data of Facebook’s European subscribers to the United States should be suspended on the ground that that country does not afford an adequate level of protection of personal data.

The original case concerns the obligations of a Data Protection Commissioner (here, the Irish one) to investigate on transatlantic data protection issues, even in light of a prior EU Commission decision that Safe Harbor provided adequate data protection.

The Complainant, Max Schrems, initiated a case alleging his fundamental rights were violated by the PRISM intelligence collection program, and the lack of recourse mechanisms.

The case was initiated in Ireland since Facebook's European headquarters are there.

FAQ #

Why care about Safe Harbor? #

A few reasons:

  • The agreement weakens the protection of fundamental rights of EU citizens
  • It is impossible for an individual to enforce the agreement on their own
  • It favors US companies as they do not need in practice to respect the same level of privacy protection
  • Because these fundamental rights will become ever more important as we transition to a knowledge economy

How many Safe Harbor requests have been filed by EU citizens? #

I don't know, and I have never been able to find precise information. This report from a US lobby gives some numbers, but the statistics are fairly limited. I have myself filed around 50 requests for access to my personal data in the past year.
